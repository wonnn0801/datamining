                                          <h1 class="title" id="page-title"> Research @ CS </h1>
                                                        <div class="tabs">  </div>

  <div class="clearfix block block-system" id="block-system-main">       <div class="content"> <article about="/research" class="node node-book clearfix" id="node-319" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document">

  
      <span class="rdf-meta element-hidden" content="Research @ CS" property="dc:title"></span>
  <div class="content">
    <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"><p><img alt="" src="http://cs.stanford.edu/sites/default/files/researchbanner.jpg" style="height:172px; width:1015px"/></p>
<h2>Core Areas</h2>
<hr/><h3 class="rteindent1"><a href="/research/ai">Stanford Artificial Intelligence Laboratory</a></h3>
<p class="rteindent1">Founded in 1962, The Stanford Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (SAIL) has been a center of excellence for Artificial Intelligence research, teaching, theory, and practice for over fifty years.</p>
<p class="rteindent1"><a href="http://ai.stanford.edu" target="_blank">Main AI Lab Page</a></p>
<p class="rteindent1"><span style="font-size:15px"><strong>Areas: </strong><a href="/research/bio">Computational Biology</a> | <a href="/research/vision">Computer Vision</a> | <a href="/research/machinelearning">Machine Learning</a> | <a href="/research/nlp">Natural Language Processing</a> | <a href="/research/robotics">Robotics</a></span></p>
<hr/><h3 class="rteindent1"><a href="/research/infolab">Stanford Infolab</a></h3>
<p class="rteindent1">Database research, general purpose network analysis and graph mining.</p>
<hr/><h3 class="rteindent1"><a href="/research/theory">Stanford Theory Group</a></h3>
<p class="rteindent1">The development and analysis of algorithms for a variety of settings and applications. Major directions include Complexity Theory, Cryptography, Algorithmic Game Theory, Geometric Algorithms, algorithmic techniques for internet applications, various aspects of network and computer security, databases, design and analysis of programs and programming languages, and supporting studies in combinatorial, logical, and algebraic mathematics.</p>
<hr/><h3 class="rteindent1"><a href="/research/graphics">Stanford Computer Graphics</a> / <a href="/research/hci">Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) group</a></h3>
<p class="rteindent1">Research in geometric computation, light fields and computational photography, human-computer interaction and design thinking as well as computational algorithms for a variety of applications.</p>
<hr/><h3 class="rteindent1"><a href="/research/computer-systems">Computer Systems</a></h3>
<p class="rteindent1">Computational techniques are now a major innovation catalyst for all aspects of human endeavour. Our research aims to develop tomorrow’s information technology that supports innovative applications, from big data analytics to the Internet of Things. It covers all aspects of information technology, including energy efficient and robust hardware systems, software defined networks, secure distributed systems, data science, and integrated circuits and power electronics.</p>
<p class="rteindent1"><span style="font-size:15px"><strong>Areas: </strong><a href="/research/architecture">Architecture</a> | <a href="/research/security">Computer Security</a> | <a href="/research/networking">Networking</a> | <a href="/research/os">Operating/Distributed Systems</a> | <a href="/research/programming">Programming Systems and Verification</a></span></p>
<hr/><p> </p>
